• Will joining a fraternity affect my academic performance?

    Yes, in order to be eligible to join a fraternity, a student must have a minimum GPA of 2.5. Academics are very important to the fraternity community and due to this, every organization must provide help to their members should they struggle to maintain this minimum.

  • I've heard about hazing in fraternities. Does that happen?

    No, hazing does not happen in RPI fraternities. We are fully committed to providing a safe, welcoming, and respectful environment for all members. We strictly adhere to the policies and laws that prohibit hazing, both from RPI Policy and the local and federal laws. Hazing is not only illegal but is counterproductive to our goal of fostering brotherhood and personal development.

  • What are the financial obligations of joining a fraternity?

    Joining a fraternity does come with financial obligations, but these costs often provide significant value. The fees typically cover membership dues, social events, and philanthropy projects, among other things. Notably, the room and board expenses are generally more affordable than those offered by RPI. Specific financial obligations vary by chapter; students are encouraged to ask individual organizations should they wish to know more.

  • What leadership opportunities are available within the fraternity system?

    Leadership opportunities within the fraternity system abound at both the chapter and council levels. At the chapter level, members can serve in roles like President or Treasurer, influencing the direction and success of the fraternity. Council leadership roles allow for broader impact within the fraternity community, shaping initiatives that benefit all chapters. These positions provide valuable experiences in teamwork, communication, and strategic thinking, enhancing both your fraternity experience and future professional endeavors.

  • How can I balance fraternity commitments with my academic and personal life?

    Balancing fraternity commitments with academic and personal life is entirely possible with proper time management and prioritization. Being part of a fraternity means joining a supportive community that values academics and personal well-being. Many chapters offer study sessions, academic resources, and mentorship to help you succeed academically. Clear communication with fraternity leadership about your needs and setting realistic expectations can further ensure a healthy balance.

  • What opportunities does the fraternity provide for personal growth and development?

    The fraternity provides diverse opportunities for personal growth and development that extend beyond social connections. Through involvement in chapter leadership, council roles, philanthropic projects, and community service, members can cultivate essential life skills such as leadership, teamwork, communication, and empathy. Engaging in these opportunities not only enriches the college experience but fosters a well-rounded individual ready to succeed in life beyond university.
